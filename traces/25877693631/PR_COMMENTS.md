## Rationale

The new term captures the specific molecular function of proteins that provide the initial point of contact between vesicle and target membranes during intracellular trafficking. This is a well-established biological process distinct from subsequent docking and fusion steps. The term appropriately inherits from "membrane-membrane adaptor activity" since tethering factors function as adaptors that physically bridge two membrane surfaces.

The parent term update to include "vesicle" aligns with the biological reality that membrane-membrane adaptor activities can establish or maintain the localization not just of proteins and organelles, but also of vesicles during trafficking events.
